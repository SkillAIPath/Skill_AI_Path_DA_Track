EduFin Collection Crisis

Strategic Problem Analysis & War Room Framework

30-Day Resolution Required

Executive Summary

Crisis Overview: EduFin is experiencing a severe collection crisis with efficiency dropping to 47% (26 percentage points below industry average). With ₹85 crores in overdue loans and a 30-day deadline to resolve the issue, immediate strategic intervention is required to prevent outsourcing at an annual cost of ₹12 crores.

Financial Impact: Current performance gaps result in estimated revenue losses of ₹2.8 crores monthly. Operational inefficiencies are costing an additional ₹897 per recovery compared to industry benchmarks.

Strategic Imperative: A cross-functional war room has been established to redesign collection processes, implement predictive analytics, optimize channel effectiveness, and create robust customer segmentation within a ₹50 lakh budget constraint.

Board Decision Pending: Failure to achieve measurable improvement within 30 days will result in outsourcing decision and ₹12 crore annual cost burden

Problem Definition

Core Issue

EduFin's collection operations have deteriorated significantly across all key performance indicators, threatening the company's financial stability and competitive position in the education finance sector. The crisis encompasses operational inefficiencies, inadequate customer segmentation, suboptimal channel utilization, and lack of predictive capabilities.

  • Willingness Assessment: Behavioral scoring and payment intent analysis
  • Ability Evaluation: Financial capacity and affordability modeling
  • Complexity Mapping: Case difficulty and resolution pathway optimization
  • Resource Allocation: Dynamic assignment based on segment profiles

  • Conversion Optimization: Channel-specific success rate improvement
  • Cost Efficiency: ROI analysis across digital and human touchpoints
  • Frequency Calibration: Optimal contact cadence per segment

  • Resolution Probability: ML models for case outcome prediction

  • Efficiency Optimization: Workflow streamlining and cycle time reduction

Constraints & Risks

Operational Constraints

  • Budget Limitation: ₹50 lakh maximum investment for technology and process improvements
  • Timeline Pressure: 30-day deadline for measurable results and board presentation
  • Real-time Delivery: War room sessions require immediate analysis and decision-making capability
  • Cross-functional Coordination: Multiple stakeholders with potentially conflicting priorities

Strategic Risks

  • Outsourcing Threat: ₹12 crore annual cost if internal resolution fails
  • Regulatory Compliance: RBI guidelines must be maintained throughout process changes
  • Customer Relationship Impact: Aggressive collection methods may damage brand reputation
  • Implementation Complexity: Technology integration within compressed timeline
  • Change Management: Staff resistance to new processes and performance metrics
